# IT Governance Redesign – Gynae360

## Objective  
To establish a structured IT governance framework that improves decision-making, aligns IT with business strategy, strengthens risk management, and supports scalability within a regulated healthcare environment.

## Governance Design Principles  

The redesigned governance model is based on the following principles:

- Alignment of IT initiatives with clinical and business objectives  
- Clear separation of decision rights and accountability  
- Formalization of governance structures and processes  
- Integration of risk management and data protection controls  
- Support for scalability and operational resilience  

## Target Governance Model  

A hybrid governance model is proposed, combining centralized oversight with distributed execution:

- **Business Monarchy** — Strategic direction led by executive leadership  
- **IT Monarchy** — Technical authority managed by IT function/provider  
- **Duopoly** — Shared decision-making between business and IT for key initiatives  

## Governance Structure  

### 1. IT Steering Committee  

**Purpose**  
Provide strategic oversight, ensure alignment between IT and business objectives, and prioritise technology investments.

**Responsibilities**  
- Approve IT strategy and roadmap  
- Prioritize major IT initiatives and investments  
- Monitor project delivery and performance  
- Oversee risk and compliance alignment  

**Proposed Members**  
- Chief Executive Officer (Chair)  
- Board Representative  
- IT Service Provider (or CIO-equivalent)  
- Clinical Representative  
- Operations Representative

### 2. IT Architecture Governance  

**Purpose**  
Ensure consistency, scalability, and integration across all systems.

**Responsibilities**  
- Define and maintain system architecture standards  
- Approve infrastructure and integration decisions  
- Ensure compatibility across platforms  
- Reduce technical debt and fragmentation

### 3. IT Investment & Project Governance  

**Purpose**  
Formalize evaluation and prioritization of IT initiatives.

**Responsibilities**  
- Establish criteria for IT investment decisions (cost, risk, value)  
- Review vendor proposals and solutions  
- Prioritize projects based on business impact  
- Monitor project execution and delivery outcomes  

### 4. IT Risk & Compliance Governance  

**Purpose**  
Strengthen cybersecurity, operational risk management, and regulatory compliance.

**Responsibilities**  
- Develop and enforce IT policies and procedures  
- Implement access control and security frameworks  
- Monitor system risks and vulnerabilities  
- Oversee incident response and recovery processes  
- Ensure compliance with applicable regulations (e.g. JDPA)

## Decision Rights Framework  

The redesigned model introduces clear allocation of decision authority:

| Decision Area | Primary Owner | Supporting Role |
|--------------|--------------|----------------|
| IT Strategy | CEO / Board | IT Steering Committee |
| Infrastructure | IT Function | Steering Committee |
| Software Selection | Shared (Business + IT) | IT Function |
| Risk & Security | IT Function | Executive Oversight |

## Governance Mechanisms  

The following mechanisms are introduced to operationalize governance:

- Formal IT strategy and roadmap  
- Defined IT investment approval process  
- Documented policies and procedures  
- Performance measurement framework (KPIs)  
- Risk register and monitoring processes  
- Structured escalation and reporting channels  

## Expected Outcomes  

Implementation of the proposed governance framework will result in:

- Improved transparency and consistency in decision-making  
- Stronger alignment between IT and business strategy  
- Reduced operational and cybersecurity risk  
- Increased accountability across IT functions  
- Enhanced scalability and support for future growth  

## Summary  

The redesigned IT governance model transitions Gynae360 from an informal, centralized structure to a formalized governance environment with defined roles, processes, and oversight mechanisms. This provides a foundation for improved operational performance, risk management, and regulatory compliance.
