# Stakeholder Interview Summary – Gynae360

## Objective  
To understand how IT decisions are made, how governance operates in practice, and identify gaps in structure, accountability, and control.

## Decision-Making Structure  
IT decision-making is informal and lacks defined governance structures.

- Final authority varies depending on context  
- Systems Administrator handles infrastructure and basic software decisions  
- CEO retains authority over critical systems and high-impact decisions  
- No formally documented decision rights or governance framework  

Decisions are escalated to executive level only when:

- they affect core business operations  
- costs exceed expected thresholds  

## Budgeting and Financial Control  

- No formal IT budgeting process exists  
- Spending is reactive and based on immediate needs  
- Procurement is driven by vendor quotes rather than structured planning  
- No involvement in strategic financial planning for IT  
- No tracking of ROI or value from IT investments  

## Architecture and Infrastructure  

- No documented IT architecture  
- Integration decisions are based on perceived compatibility rather than standards  
- Cybersecurity approach is proactive in intent but lacks formal governance structure  
- Backups are performed and tested regularly  

## Application Governance  

- No formal process for initiating or approving new applications  
- Vendor risk assessments are conducted informally  
- No structured user acceptance testing process  
- No defined change management framework  

## Risk and Compliance  

- Data Protection Act is recognized as the primary regulatory framework  
- No formal risk register or documented risk management process  
- No penetration testing or structured vulnerability assessments  
- Access rights are controlled centrally by the CEO  
- Segregation of duties exists but is not formally governed  

## Governance Mechanisms  

- No IT steering committee  
- No formal IT policies or procedures  
- No policy review cycles  
- No structured communication of IT priorities across the organization  

## Performance and Monitoring  

- No KPIs or performance metrics for IT  
- No incident tracking or analysis  
- No user satisfaction measurement  

## Key Governance Risks  

- Over-reliance on centralized decision-making  
- Lack of formal governance structures and controls  
- Absence of performance visibility  
- Increased exposure to cybersecurity and operational risks  
- Limited scalability as the organization grows  

## Summary  

The current IT environment operates with minimal governance structure, relying heavily on informal processes and executive oversight. While functional at a small scale, this model introduces significant risks related to control, accountability, and long-term sustainability.
