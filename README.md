# IT Governance Assessment & Redesign – Gynae360 (Healthcare Sector)

## Overview

This case study presents an assessment of the existing IT governance environment at Gynae360, a private healthcare provider in Jamaica, and outlines a proposed redesign to improve governance maturity, decision-making effectiveness, and alignment between IT and business strategy.

The engagement focused on evaluating current governance practices, identifying structural and operational gaps, and defining a governance framework capable of supporting organizational growth, regulatory compliance, and improved service delivery.

## Scope of Work

The assessment covered the following areas:

* Evaluation of existing IT governance structures and decision-making processes
* Analysis of stakeholder roles, responsibilities, and decision rights
* Review of IT investment, budgeting, and prioritization practices
* Assessment of IT architecture, infrastructure governance, and vendor dependency
* Identification of governance gaps and associated operational and risk implications
* Development of a structured IT governance framework aligned with business objectives
* Consideration of data protection requirements under the Jamaica Data Protection Act (JDPA)

## Key Findings

The current IT governance environment is informal and highly centralized, with limited structure to support scalable and consistent decision-making.

Key issues identified include:

* Absence of formal IT governance structures, including steering committees and defined governance bodies
* Centralization of decision-making authority within executive leadership, with limited formal delegation
* Lack of documented IT policies, procedures, and governance standards
* Informal and ad hoc approach to IT investment evaluation and vendor selection
* Absence of documented IT architecture and system integration standards
* No defined performance measurement framework, including KPIs or service metrics
* Limited alignment between IT initiatives and broader organizational strategy

These conditions introduce risks related to decision consistency, operational efficiency, scalability, and regulatory compliance.

## Governance Redesign (Summary)

A structured IT governance model was proposed to address identified gaps and improve overall governance effectiveness.

The redesigned framework includes:

* Establishment of an IT Steering Committee to provide strategic oversight and alignment with business objectives
* Definition of governance structures across key domains, including architecture, investment, and risk management
* Introduction of formal decision-making processes with clearly defined roles and responsibilities
* Implementation of IT policy and risk governance mechanisms, including cybersecurity and data protection controls
* Development of structured IT investment evaluation and prioritization processes
* Introduction of performance measurement mechanisms to track IT value delivery and operational effectiveness

The proposed model adopts a hybrid governance approach, combining centralized strategic oversight with delegated technical decision-making.
