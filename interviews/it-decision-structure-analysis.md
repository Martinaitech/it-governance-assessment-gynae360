# IT Decision Structure Analysis – Gynae360

## Overview  
This analysis evaluates how IT decisions are currently made within Gynae360 and identifies structural weaknesses impacting governance effectiveness.

## Decision Authority Breakdown  

### Infrastructure Decisions  
- Managed by Systems Administrator  
- No formal review or governance process  

### Software Decisions  
- Split authority depending on system type  
- Administrative tools handled by Systems Administrator  
- Core business systems approved by CEO  

### Strategic Decisions  
- Centralized at CEO level  
- No formal governance body or advisory structure  

## Observations  

- Decision-making authority is fragmented and inconsistent  
- No documented governance model defining decision rights  
- Heavy reliance on individual roles rather than structured processes  
- Lack of separation between strategic, tactical, and operational decisions  


## Escalation Model  

Decisions are escalated based on:

- financial impact  
- operational disruption  

There is no defined escalation framework or thresholds.

## Governance Gaps  

- No formal IT governance structure  
- No IT Steering Committee  
- No defined decision-making framework  
- No documentation of roles and responsibilities  
- No accountability mechanisms for decisions  

## Risk Implications  

- Inconsistent decision quality  
- Increased operational risk due to lack of oversight  
- Limited transparency and accountability  
- Dependency on key individuals  
- Reduced ability to scale IT operations  

## Recommended Structure  

Introduce a formal governance model with:

- IT Steering Committee for strategic oversight  
- Defined decision rights across business and IT functions  
- Structured escalation framework  
- Clear separation of strategic, tactical, and operational decisions  

## Conclusion  

The current decision-making model is informal and highly centralized, limiting governance effectiveness. Establishing a structured governance framework is critical to improving control, accountability, and alignment with business objectives.
